The Mesopotamian Seismological Network (MPSN) was initiated by Haydar Al-Shukri and Hanan Mahdi of the University of Arkansas at Little Rock (UA Little Rock) in collaboration with Lawrence Livermore National Lab (LLNL). The project was initially supported by funding from the National Science Foundation, US DoE, and UA Little Rock. The project included the installation of broadband seismic stations, training, and capacity building for research and infrastructure. All stations were installed in cooperation with the Universities of Basrah, Baghdad, Sumer, Anbar, Sulaymaniyah, Kirkuk, and Duhok. The Iraqi team consists of researchers from these universities. The Seismological Laboratory of the University of Basrah (SLUB) was established in 2014 at the Department of Geology, College of Science, University of Basra. SLUB is the data center of the MPSN.
